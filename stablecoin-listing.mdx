# Stablecoin listing guide

BlankFX is designed to support multiple currency corridors. Each new stablecoin listed creates a new FX pair and expands the protocol's reach. This guide describes the requirements and due diligence process for adding a new stablecoin.

## Listing requirements

### 1. Regulatory and legal

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Issuer jurisdiction | Issuer must be domiciled in a jurisdiction with clear stablecoin regulations (e.g. EU under MiCA, US state-licensed, Singapore MAS-licensed) |
| License/registration | Issuer must hold relevant money transmission, e-money, or stablecoin-specific license |
| Reserve attestation | Monthly or quarterly reserve reports from an independent auditor or accounting firm |
| Legal opinion | Legal opinion confirming the token is not classified as a security in its primary jurisdiction |
| Terms of service | Published terms covering redemption rights, liability, and user protections |

### 2. Reserve quality

| Criterion | Minimum standard |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Reserve composition | 100% backed by cash, cash equivalents, or short-term government securities |
| Custody | Reserves held at regulated financial institutions, segregated from issuer operating funds |
| Audit frequency | At minimum quarterly attestation by a reputable accounting firm |
| Redemption | 1:1 redemption available to verified holders within a reasonable timeframe (ideally T+0 to T+2) |
| Transparency | Public disclosure of reserve composition, custodian identity, and attestation reports |

### 3. Technical

| Criterion | Minimum standard |
|-----------|-----------------|
| ERC-20 compliance | Standard ERC-20 interface on Ethereum (or target chain) |
| Contract verification | Source code verified on Etherscan or equivalent block explorer |
| Audit | Smart contract audited by a recognized security firm |
| Decimals | 6 or 18 decimals (must be consistent and documented) |
| Pausability | If the contract has pause/freeze functionality, the governance process for triggering it must be documented |
| Proxy pattern | If upgradeable, the upgrade governance and timelock must be documented |
| Blacklist | If the contract has blacklist functionality, the policy for blacklisting addresses must be disclosed |

### 4. Market and liquidity

| Criterion | Minimum standard |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Market cap | Minimum $10M in circulation |
| Exchange listings | Listed on at least 2 reputable centralized exchanges or DEXs |
| On-chain liquidity | Existing DEX liquidity of at least $1M TVL |
| Oracle feed | Price feed available from a recognized oracle provider (Chainlink, Pyth, Redstone) or reliable on-chain TWAP |
| Trading history | At least 6 months of continuous trading activity |

## Due diligence process

### Step 1: Application

The stablecoin issuer (or a sponsor) submits a listing proposal including:
- Token details (name, symbol, address, decimals, chain)
- Issuer identity and jurisdiction
- License/registration documentation
- Latest reserve attestation report
- Smart contract audit report
- Oracle feed details

### Step 2: Technical review

BlankFX team reviews:
- Smart contract code and audit findings
- ERC-20 compliance and edge cases (fee-on-transfer, rebasing, etc.)
- Oracle feed reliability and update frequency
- Pausability and admin key risks

### Step 3: Risk assessment

Evaluation of:
- Reserve quality and custodian reputation
- Regulatory risk in target jurisdictions
- Concentration risk (single custodian, single bank, etc.)
- Depeg history and incident response track record
- Issuer financial health (if public information available)

### Step 4: Pool configuration

If approved:
- New FX Pool deployed for the currency
- Oracle feed configured and tested
- Spread parameters calibrated based on liquidity depth and volatility
- Pool added to the Router whitelist
- Frontend updated to show the new currency

### Step 5: Liquidity bootstrapping

Initial liquidity is seeded through:
- Issuer partnership (issuer deposits initial liquidity)
- LP incentive program (boosted fee share for early depositors)
- Co-marketing with the issuer to attract depositors

## Rejection criteria

A stablecoin will not be listed if:
- The issuer cannot provide reserve attestations from an independent auditor
- The smart contract has unresolved critical or high-severity audit findings
- The token has experienced a depeg exceeding 5% that was not resolved within 48 hours
- The issuer is domiciled in a sanctioned jurisdiction
- The token uses a fee-on-transfer or rebasing mechanism that is incompatible with BlankFX pool accounting
- No reliable oracle feed exists for the underlying currency pair
