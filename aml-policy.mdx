# AML/CFT policy

<Note>
This is a summary of BlankFX's Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing policy. The full policy document is maintained internally and available to regulators and compliance partners on request.
</Note>

## Commitment

BlankFX is committed to preventing the use of its protocol for money laundering, terrorist financing, sanctions evasion, or other financial crimes. This policy applies to all users, liquidity providers, market makers, and integration partners.

## Risk-based approach

BlankFX applies a risk-based approach to AML/CFT compliance, as recommended by the Financial Action Task Force (FATF). This means:
- Higher-risk activities and users receive enhanced scrutiny
- Lower-risk activities follow simplified procedures
- Risk assessments are reviewed and updated regularly

## User access and identification

All users must be registered through BlankFX's access control system before trading. Access tiers determine:
- Maximum trade sizes per transaction and per day
- Available features (pool swaps, RFQ access, LP deposits)
- Reporting and monitoring thresholds

The protocol's on-chain access control (BlankFXAccess contract) enforces these tiers at the smart contract level. Suspended users cannot execute any transactions.

## Transaction monitoring

BlankFX monitors on-chain activity for:
- Unusual transaction patterns (rapid-fire trades, round-number amounts)
- Transactions involving flagged addresses (OFAC SDN list, other sanctions lists)
- Volume thresholds that trigger enhanced review
- Structuring patterns designed to avoid reporting thresholds

## Sanctions compliance

BlankFX screens all wallet addresses against:
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list
- EU consolidated sanctions list
- UN Security Council sanctions list
- Other applicable sanctions regimes

Wallets associated with sanctioned individuals, entities, or jurisdictions are blocked from accessing the protocol.

## Suspicious activity reporting

When suspicious activity is identified, BlankFX:
1. Freezes the relevant account (access tier set to suspended)
2. Documents the activity and supporting evidence
3. Files a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with the relevant financial intelligence unit
4. Cooperates with law enforcement as required

## Record keeping

BlankFX maintains records of:
- User registration and access tier assignments
- All on-chain transactions (inherently preserved on the blockchain)
- Monitoring alerts and their resolution
- SARs filed and law enforcement requests received

Records are retained for a minimum of 5 years after the end of the business relationship.

## Training

All team members with access to user data or compliance systems receive AML/CFT training at onboarding and annually thereafter.

## Policy review

This policy is reviewed at least annually and updated to reflect:
- Changes in applicable regulations
- New risks identified through monitoring
- Guidance from regulators and industry bodies
