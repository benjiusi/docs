# Pricing mechanism

## Rate calculation

Every BlankFX swap follows the same pricing formula:

```
Output = Input × Oracle Rate × (1 - Spread)
```

**Oracle Rate** comes from on-chain price feeds that track real-world FX markets. For EUR/USD, this is the current interbank mid-rate published by the oracle provider.

**Spread** is the protocol's fee. It varies by pair type:

| Pair type | Typical spread | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| USD ↔ USD (e.g. USDC → USDT) | ~0.05% | Minimal risk, same currency |
| Cross-currency (e.g. USDC → EURC) | 0.10–0.30% | FX risk + oracle cost |

## How this differs from AMM pricing

![AMM vs BlankFX pricing](/images/pricing-comparison.svg)

The practical difference: a $1M swap on BlankFX gets the same rate as a $100 swap. On a standard AMM, the $1M swap would move the price significantly against you.

## Gasless pricing

When you use gasless mode, the relay service pays Ethereum gas on your behalf. The gas cost is recovered by slightly widening the spread.

```
Gasless output = Input × Oracle Rate × (1 - Spread - Gas Recovery)
```

Gas recovery is dynamic — it adjusts based on current Ethereum gas prices. On low-gas days, you barely notice it. On high-gas days, it increases. The interface always shows you the final output amount with gas included, so there are no surprises.

There is a minimum swap size for gasless mode. If the swap is too small, the gas cost would eat too much of the spread. The interface will tell you if your swap doesn't meet the minimum.

## Rate freshness

Oracle rates update on a regular cadence (typically every few seconds to minutes, depending on the feed). The protocol uses the latest available rate at the time your transaction executes.

There is a deadline on every swap. If your transaction doesn't execute before the deadline (default: 20 minutes), it reverts and you keep your tokens. This prevents stale rates from being used against you.

## Fee distribution

100% of spread revenue goes to liquidity providers. The protocol does not take a separate platform fee. This means:

- USD Pool LPs earn from every USD ↔ USD swap AND the USD side of every cross-currency swap
- EUR Pool LPs earn from every swap that touches the EUR Pool

USD Pool LPs effectively earn from all trading activity on the protocol, making it the highest-yield position.
